Board of Directors

Our Governance Structure

Our board provides strategic direction and ensures the institute upholds its mission, independence, and values.

Hloriso N. Mohale

Head of Research & Digital Policy Framework

Hloriso N. Mohale is a software developer and cross-platform systems architect, and a member of the BRICS Council. His work bridges hands-on engineering with multilateral digital strategy, focusing on digital governance, AI policy frameworks, and digital sovereignty. He participates in the Digital Economy, Digital Sovereignty, Artificial Intelligence and Governance working group at the BRICS Council and has also contributed to policy development under G20 WG13 – Digital and Inclusive Economies, supporting initiatives that advance inclusive digital transformation and responsible technology governance.

Alandile Manyiwana

Head of Finance & Administration

Alandile brings a strong foundation in financial services to the Digital Sovereignty Institute, with expertise spanning credit risk management, client engagement, and portfolio performance. His career in the financial sector has equipped him with sharp analytical capabilities and a nuanced understanding of regulatory compliance and ethical service delivery. At the Institute, he applies his stakeholder management skills to advancing responsible digital governance — bridging the gap between policy and practice in an increasingly complex digital economy.

Governance Principles

How We Are Governed

The board provides strategic oversight while maintaining the institute's independence from government and commercial interests.

Independence

The institute operates independently of government, political parties, and commercial funders.

Transparency

Our funding sources, research methods, and governance processes are publicly disclosed.

Accountability

The board is accountable to the institute's membership and the public interest mission.
