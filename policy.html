Our Approach

How We Advocate

We engage government, academia, civil society, and international bodies to advance responsible digital governance.

01

Research-Driven Policy Recommendations

Every advocacy position we take is grounded in rigorous, independent research. We do not advocate for political positions — we advocate for evidence-based policy that protects constitutional rights and serves the public interest.

02

Engagement with Government

We submit formal comments to Parliament, the Information Regulator, the Department of Communications and Digital Technologies, and other government bodies on legislation and policy affecting digital governance, AI, and cybersecurity.

03

Civil Society Collaboration

We work alongside civil society organisations, community groups, and public interest advocates to ensure that digital governance policy reflects the needs and rights of all South Africans — not just the technologically powerful.

04

Academic & International Engagement

We engage with South African universities, African Union bodies, UN agencies, and international digital governance forums to ensure South African and African perspectives are represented in global policy conversations.

05

Public Awareness & Education

We produce accessible content — articles, briefings, and commentary — to help ordinary South Africans understand their digital rights and the governance challenges that affect them.
