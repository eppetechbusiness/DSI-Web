About the Institute

A South African policy and research institute dedicated to governing the digital future responsibly.

Why the Digital Sovereignty Institute Exists

South Africa and the broader African continent are navigating a digital transformation that is moving faster than the governance frameworks designed to manage it. Laws are outdated. Regulators are under-resourced. Citizens are largely unaware of their digital rights.

The Digital Sovereignty Institute was founded to close that gap. We are an independent, non-profit policy and research institute that produces evidence-based research, develops practical policy recommendations, and engages government, civil society, and the private sector on the most critical digital governance challenges of our time.

We believe that Africa must have its own voice in shaping the global rules of the digital economy — rules that reflect African values, African realities, and African ambitions.

Our Core Values

  • Independence — we are not funded by or beholden to any government or corporation
  • Evidence-based — all our work is grounded in rigorous research
  • Africa-centred — we develop solutions suited to African contexts
  • Accessible — we write for policymakers, academics, and citizens alike
  • Constitutional — we are committed to the rights enshrined in South Africa's Constitution
  • Collaborative — we work with partners across government, academia, and civil society
Our Purpose

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

The Digital Sovereignty Institute is a policy-focused institute dedicated to advancing responsible digital transformation, ethical AI governance, cybersecurity resilience, and forward-looking digital policy in South Africa and the Global South. We support government, institutions, and society through research, policy recommendations, publications, and capacity-building initiatives.

Our Vision

To contribute to a digitally resilient, inclusive, and well-governed society where technology advances national development, public trust, and constitutional values — and where Africa leads rather than follows in shaping the global digital order.
